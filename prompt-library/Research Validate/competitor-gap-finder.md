---
title: Competitor Gap Finder
---

## When to use
Before writing a single line of code, when you need to validate your differentiation and understand the real gaps in the market you're entering.

## Prompt

```
Help me analyze my competitive landscape before I start building. I will name 3 direct competitors. For each one, I need a structured breakdown.

My product idea: [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCT IN 2 SENTENCES]

Competitors to analyze:
1. [COMPETITOR 1 NAME + URL]
2. [COMPETITOR 2 NAME + URL]
3. [COMPETITOR 3 NAME + URL]

For each competitor, provide:

A. WHAT THEY DO WELL: 3 specific things this product genuinely does well. Be concrete , not "good UX" but "onboarding completes in under 60 seconds with no credit card required."

B. WHERE THEY CONSISTENTLY FAIL: Based on public user reviews (G2, Trustpilot, Reddit, App Store, Product Hunt comments), what do users repeatedly complain about? List 3 specific, recurring complaints. These are the gaps.

C. WHO THEY ARE OPTIMIZED FOR: Describe the exact user type this product is built for. Not a demographic , a specific person with a specific workflow.

D. WHAT THEY IGNORE: What user type or use case does this competitor consistently ignore or handle poorly?

After analyzing all 3, output:

GAP ANALYSIS: A list of 3-5 specific gaps that appear across multiple competitors , problems that are underserved across the entire market, not just one player.

DIFFERENTIATION STATEMENT: One sentence in this format: "Unlike [Competitor A] and [Competitor B], [MY PRODUCT] is the only [category] that [specific differentiator] for [specific user]." Make it falsifiable and specific , if it could describe any product, rewrite it until it couldn't.
```
